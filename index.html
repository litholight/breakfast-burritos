Sean's Breakfast Burritos

  • Use whole wheat or corn tortillas for added fiber
  • Use lean protein sources such as turkey bacon or chicken sausage
  • Incorporate a variety of vegetables, such as spinach, bell peppers, and onions, for added nutrients and flavor
  • Use low-fat cheese or avocado as a topping for added creaminess without the extra fat
  • Use egg whites or a low-fat dairy alternative instead of full-fat eggs
Here's a step-by-step procedure for cooking and freezing breakfast burritos:
  1. Cook your filling ingredients in a skillet. For example, you can saut√© diced vegetables, diced potatoes, and lean protein in a skillet.
  2. Cook your eggs in a separate skillet. If you're using egg whites or a low-fat dairy alternative, cook them as you would scrambled eggs.
  3. Assemble your burritos by placing a few spoonfuls of the filling on a tortilla, and then adding the scrambled eggs on top. Add some cheese or avocado if desired.
  4. Roll the burrito up, tucking in the sides as you go.
  5. Once all the burritos are assembled, wrap them tightly in aluminum foil.
  6. Place the wrapped burritos in a resealable plastic bag or container and put them in the freezer.
  7. To reheat, remove the foil and wrap the burrito in a damp paper towel. Heat it in the microwave for 2-3 minutes, or until heated through.
It's also worth mentioning that breakfast burritos can be reheated in the oven too, but that will take longer than microwaving.
You can also try freezing the ingredients separately and assemble them when you are ready to eat.
